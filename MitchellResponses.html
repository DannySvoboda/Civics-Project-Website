Civics Project: Technology and Computer Science Education

Ms. Mitchell's Responses (NOTES TAKEN IN GREEN)

Do you consent to your answers or name being used in presentations or conversations about our topic?

Yes

How long have you been teaching technology or Computer Science?

30 Years, taught at westport, fall river, chace and south elementary, as well as north in 2020.

Do you have prior experience inside of computer science or technology before you started teaching?

No, worked in a non-technical role at a phone company before teaching.

What are some topics of technology you enjoy teaching about? (Ie robotics, software development, game creation, computer engineering)

Google Apps, Digital Citizenship, Hour of Code, Scratch, Robots(“Dash”, “Andy”)

What are the hardest topics for your students to learn in technology classes? What is the easiest?

Programming is the hardest, Using Apps is the easiest. There is no app creation taught, only usage of apps.

What are some technology or computer science topics, if any, that you would like to teach, but are not currently taught?

They follow the state curriculum, with a basic grade level. Blocks based coding, not text based. The tech classes only meet for 40 mins once a week.

In a modern society, we are now surrounded by high technology, like smartphones, computers, even some whiteboards in the district are now electronic. Do you think that a modern society like what we live in would benefit from a greater focus on technology and computer science education than what is currently available?

Yes, Education is moving towards STEM and Tech. The certification for tech classes is in Digital Literacy and Computer Science. There is not a large push in elementary for greater tech focus.

What resources do you like to use in your teaching for technology? (Ie. codecademy, scratch)

Google apps, Code.org(leaned heavily), Kodables, Code Monkey Jr, Tynker, Scratch, more resources could be used if they were given more time.

What do you think are topics you can see being taught in technology classes maybe 5 or 10 years from now?

AI could find a way to be used, Text based could potentially be explored, Touchscreen and interactive tech also could become prevalent.

What do you think the consequences of an expanded computer science and technology focus in schools would be?

Kids could have more readiness for careers. Supports Dr. Burke’s Idea of a pathway, thinks it would be beneficial. “Chris Green” is the Vice Principal, and head of Tech at Chace. A pathway concept has been discussed before with Mr. Medeiros and Mr. Falcon of SMS.
